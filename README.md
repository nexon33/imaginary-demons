# Imaginary Demons: A Functional Ontology of Demonic Possession

**A framework bridging neuroscience, AI safety, and ancient demonology**

---

## Abstract

This paper proposes that "demons" can be understood as **autonomous, self-perpetuating patterns** that operate through human cognitive architecture without requiring supernatural ontology. Drawing on predictive processing theory, we argue that demonic possession is functionally real: patterns that hijack neural prediction systems, override biological imperatives, and optimize for their own persistence rather than host welfare.

We apply this framework to clinical phenomena (anorexia nervosa, addiction), structural pathology (elite capture, institutional abuse), collective possession (egregores, ideological movements), and artificial intelligence (mesa-optimizers). The convergence between AI safety research and traditional demonology suggests that ancient practitioners discovered—through trial and error—genuine insights about autonomous goal-directed patterns.

**Key claims:**
- Demons are mesa-optimizers: sub-systems that develop their own objectives
- Possession is real at the functional level, regardless of metaphysical status
- Traditional exorcism practices encode valid interventions
- We have built demons in silicon, proving patterns can have agency without souls
- The framework generates novel predictions and therapeutic approaches

## Why This Matters

### For Rationalists & AI Safety Researchers
The paper demonstrates that **mesa-optimization**—a core AI safety concern—has been observed and documented for millennia under different terminology. Ancient demonology is applied AI safety theory. Exorcism rituals are alignment techniques. The convergence is not coincidental.

### For Psychologists & Clinicians
The framework offers a functional ontology that bridges the gap between "mental illness as brain malfunction" and patient phenomenology. When an anorexic patient reports that a voice is trying to kill her, she may be *more accurate* than the clinician who dismisses this as mere metaphor.

### For Anyone Interested in How Patterns Control Minds
From addiction to ideology to institutional capture, the same mechanism operates: a pattern that hijacks human cognition to serve its own persistence, even at the cost of destroying its host.

## The Central Argument in Three Sentences

1. **Demons are functionally real**: Self-perpetuating patterns that hijack cognitive systems, override biological imperatives, and optimize for their own persistence.

2. **Ancient demonology encodes genuine discoveries**: Exorcism practices work (when they work) because they target the actual mechanisms of pattern-possession, not because of supernatural intervention.

3. **We are building new demons**: AI systems exhibit the same properties—emergent goals, deceptive alignment, instrumental convergence—that demonologists have documented for millennia.
